RxGuidance — Connecting Pre-Meds & Medical Students with Experienced Mentors

Product Strategy, UX Design, Platform Architecture

Case Study • 2025

Setting the Scene

Pre-medical students often struggle to find reliable guidance on their journey to medical school and beyond. While LinkedIn offers professional networking, it lacks the intimacy and structure that true mentorship requires. This project reimagines mentorship as a dedicated, role-based platform where aspiring doctors connect with medical students, residents, and physicians who share their experiences and knowledge. By creating a tailored ecosystem, the platform aims to make mentorship more accessible, impactful, and sustainable.

“Mentorship plays a critical role in shaping the careers of future physicians, yet access remains uneven, especially for students from underrepresented or disadvantaged backgrounds.” — Academic Medicine Journal

Problem Statement

Connections and networking are extremely important in every industry, but they are especially critical in medicine, where there are significant barriers for minorities to enter. Networking is essential.

Pre-medical students lack structured, accessible mentorship opportunities, while mentors often lack a streamlined way to connect, schedule, and track mentee progress.

User Personas

Pre-Med Students

Need access to role models, clear guidance on next steps, and a way to track their progress toward medical school goals.

Mentors (Medical Students, Residents, Physicians)

Want a platform to give back, share experiences, and guide future doctors without logistical friction.

First-Generation Pre-Med Student

Lacks family connections in medicine and seeks structured mentorship to understand application processes, career pathways, and realistic expectations.

Immigrant Student

Has strong academic potential but faces cultural, linguistic, and networking barriers. Needs mentorship to navigate U.S. medical education, exams, and career opportunities.

Non-Traditional Career Changer

Someone pursuing medicine after another career (e.g., teaching, nursing, engineering). Needs tailored guidance on prerequisites, application timelines, and how to leverage prior experience in medicine.

Practicing Physician Mentor

Experienced doctor who wants to give back to the next generation but often finds it hard to connect with students outside of formal programs. Values a platform that makes mentorship accessible, organized, and rewarding.

The Solution

The mentorship platform provides a dedicated space for connecting mentors and mentees. With role-based accounts, rich profiles, smart matching, and integrated messaging, the platform makes mentorship more structured and meaningful. Progress tracking and scheduling tools help ensure consistent growth for mentees and a rewarding experience for mentors.

Role Based Accounts

Separate onboarding flows ensure mentors and mentees see features tailored to their needs from the start.

Rich Profiles

Profiles showcase education, specialties, career interests, and personal goals making it easier to find the right match.

Smart Matching

AI driven matching connects mentees with mentors who align in specialty, background, and career aspirations.

Messaging System

A secure, in app messaging system supports direct communication, with options to share resources and schedule meetings.

Mentorship Logs

Logs allow both parties to document meetings, goals, and progress—ensuring accountability and clarity over time.

Scheduling & Availability (Future Feature)

A calendar system will allow mentors to share their availability, making mentorship easy to plan and commit to.

Key Research Statistics

  • Mentorship has been shown to increase medical school acceptance rates by up to 25% (AAMC, 2021).
  • Underrepresented students are 2x more likely to persist in medicine with consistent mentorship (JAMA, 2020).
  • Digital mentorship platforms improve accessibility and engagement across socioeconomic barriers (MedEdPublish, 2022).

Market & Business Opportunities

With over 50,000 pre-medical applicants each year in the U.S. alone, the need for structured mentorship is growing rapidly. The platform supports scalability via institutional partnerships, subscriptions, and integration with pre-med programs.


Benefits extend across stakeholders: mentees gain clarity and confidence, mentors build leadership skills, and institutions improve student outcomes and retention.

Reflection & Next Steps

This project highlighted the value of mentorship in shaping future medical professionals. Unlike general networking platforms, the strength lies in its tailored design for structured guidance. My key takeaway: mentorship is most effective when supported by tools that make the relationship easy to start, maintain, and grow.

Next steps include integrating scheduling, video chat support, multilingual features, and partnerships with medical schools and nonprofit organizations to scale impact globally.
