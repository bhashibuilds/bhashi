Born in Chennai, India and raised between cultures, I moved to the United States at the age of eight as a first-generation immigrant. Curious and a little mischievous as a child, I often took things apart and destroyed any complex gadget just to see how they worked, a habit that has grown into a love for exploring, questioning, and building. During high school, I pursued dual enrollment at PGCC in health sciences. Witnessing my grandmother’s cancer diagnosis initially drew me toward medicine, and many of my tech projects reflect a strong medical influence and a desire to create tools that truly help people. Yet, through this journey, I realized that my curiosity and drive were not limited to medicine alone — I was passionate about solving problems at their root. I came to understand that leveraging technology would allow me to make a much broader impact than pursuing medicine alone. I discovered that technology could amplify my curiosity and impact, allowing me to build tools that touch more lives and tackle a wide variety of challenges.

I enjoy learning languages, from Chinese and French to Spanish, Hindi, Telugu, and Tamil. I paint, draw, sculpt, and garden. Whether through art, technology, or connection, I am always searching for new ways to build, understand, and give back to my community.
